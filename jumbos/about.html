This submission builds on several projects I’ve worked on with the Tufts Baseball Data Operations team and Baseball Analytics at Tufts (BAT), where we analyze team and league data to create scouting reports and uncover competitive advantages. One example is my TrackMan project from the spring, where I developed a webpage to filter raw TrackMan data by team, pitcher, and hitter. This allowed us to identify a pitchers’ go-to pitches and spot opposing teams' approaches.

My goal with this app was to build a tool that could help a team. By using filters to visualize the data, we were able to uncover pitcher tendencies, patterns in strategy, and hitter weaknesses. One key insight with this page is that the data alone doesn’t always tell the full story. While certain patterns may seem clear, they need to be interpreted alongside other variables to assess their true usability and validity.
